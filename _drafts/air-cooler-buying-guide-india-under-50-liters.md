## How to Choose: Buying Decision Framework

Before diving into models, figure out what you actually need.

### Step 1: Determine Your Room Size

| Room Size                       | Recommended Capacity | Recommended Air Delivery |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Up to 100 sq ft (bedroom/cabin) | 12–20L               | 500–800 m³/hr            |
| 100–150 sq ft (bedroom)         | 20–30L               | 700–1200 m³/hr           |
| 150–200 sq ft (living room)     | 30–40L               | 1000–1800 m³/hr          |
| 200–250 sq ft (large room)      | 40–50L               | 1800–2500 m³/hr          |

### Step 2: Check Your Climate Zone

> **Important:** Air coolers work best in **dry climates** (humidity < 60%). In humid coastal cities, effectiveness drops significantly.

| Climate        | City Examples                    | Cooler Effectiveness | Recommendation      |
| -------------- | -------------------------------- | -------------------- | ------------------- |
| Hot & Dry      | Delhi, Jaipur, Nagpur, Ahmedabad | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Excellent      | Desert cooler       |
| Hot & Semi-dry | Pune, Hyderabad, Bengaluru       | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Good            | Personal/desert     |
| Hot & Humid    | Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata         | ⭐⭐ Poor              | Consider AC instead |
| Moderate       | Hill stations                    | ⭐⭐⭐ Moderate         | Personal cooler     |

### Step 3: Prioritize Features

**Must-have:**
- ✅ Honeycomb cooling pads (not wood wool)
- ✅ Inverter compatible (if power cuts are frequent)
- ✅ 4-way swing (for better air distribution)
- ✅ Water level indicator

**Good to have:** Remote control, digital display, auto-fill, castor wheels, dust/mosquito filter

**Nice to have:** Sleep mode/timer, anti-microbial pads (Blue Star), humidity control (Livpure)

## Understanding Key Specs

### Cooling Pad Type
- **Honeycomb pads** — Cross-fluted cellulose. Best cooling, lasts 3–5 years. Always preferred.
- **Wood wool / Aspen pads** — Cheaper, less efficient, needs replacement every 1–2 years. Avoid if possible.
- **Anti-microbial honeycomb** — Honeycomb with anti-bacterial coating. Found in Blue Star Aura Neo. Prevents mold buildup.

### Air Delivery (m³/hr or CFM)
Higher = more air circulation. Some brands list CFM instead of m³/hr (1 m³/hr ≈ 0.59 CFM). We've standardized to m³/hr where possible.

### Fan vs Blower
- **Fan** — Wide cone of air. Better for open/semi-open spaces and larger rooms.
- **Blower** — Focused stream. Better for closed rooms, more efficient cooling in smaller spaces.

### Inverter Compatibility
Runs on home inverter (UPS) during power cuts. Always verify your inverter can handle the cooler's wattage (typically 90–250W).

### User Ratings
We've included aggregated ratings from Flipkart, Amazon, and Smartprix. A model with 10,000+ ratings and 3.8+ stars is a safe bet. Models with fewer than 500 ratings need caution regardless of star count.

## Practical Tips

### Water Filling Methods

| Method          | How It Works                           | Found In                                            |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **Top fill**    | Pour water from top opening            | Most personal coolers                               |
| **Front fill**  | Fill from front panel                  | Symphony Diet series                                |
| **Auto fill**   | Connect to tap; float valve auto-stops | Blue Star Aura Neo 45, Symphony Sumo (select)       |
| **Ice chamber** | Dedicated tray for ice blocks          | Almost all modern coolers (except Hindware Xeno 45) |

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Cooler too small for your room** — Under-capacity cooler runs continuously without effective cooling.
2. **Fully closed room** — Air coolers need cross-ventilation. Keep a window slightly open.
3. **Ignoring humidity** — In Mumbai/Chennai, a cooler adds humidity and makes you feel hotter.
4. **Choosing wood wool pads** — Honeycomb pads last 3–5x longer and cool better. Worth the extra ₹500–1000.
5. **Not checking inverter wattage** — Ensure your inverter can handle the cooler's wattage (90–250W).
6. **Skipping castor wheels** — Essential if you move the cooler between rooms.
7. **Ignoring after-sales service** — Symphony and Crompton have the widest networks. Croma offers in-store support.
8. **Trusting low-volume ratings** — 10K+ ratings at 3.8★ is safer than 50 ratings at 4.5★.
9. **Overlooking power consumption** — 90W Livpure vs 230W Symphony saves ~₹250/month at 8 hrs/day.

### Maintenance Schedule

| Task                       | Frequency     | Why                               |
| -------------------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Clean water tank           | Every 2 weeks | Prevents algae and bad odor       |
| Clean/replace cooling pads | Every season  | Maintains cooling efficiency      |
| Clean dust filter          | Monthly       | Maintains airflow                 |
| Drain water before storage | End of season | Prevents mold and rust            |
| Lubricate fan motor        | Annually      | Reduces noise, extends life       |
| Check pump                 | Monthly       | Ensures water circulation to pads |
